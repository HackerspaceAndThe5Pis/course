Preparing CAD model for manufacturing

2.5 Now the setup is complete, we need add our tool.

From here you can enter your dimensions of your tool. The speed and feed rate also to be added here.

2.6 Next you define the toolpaths

We will generate paths the tool will use to cut the model. As seen above there’s variety of options to choose from. For this type of model 2D Contour suits better.

Select the tool you defined previously and adjust the feed rate, and the spindle rate etc. according to your own machine.

In the contour tab, we will choose what contour we are cutting. Choose all the contour you want to cut as seen below.

The next tab is called Heights. It will outline the movements of the tool, we can have them leaved as default.

The Passes tab is not required to be change for our type of model. But this is where you outline the depths of your cuts

The next tab we are looking is called the linking tab. Make sure to keep the “tool down” box unchecked.

After you have identified all your settings you can now click ok and wait for it to be rendered.

Now you can simulate the toolpath you just created. Any mistakes and errors can be identified by looking at the result after doing a simulation.
